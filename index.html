    • Recent Projects

  • CSIE: Coded strip-patterns image enhancer embedded in structured light-based methods - 项目负责人

    • Project Page

    We propose a novel and effectively coded strip-patterns image enhancer (CSIE) designed for structured light systems. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first time that CSIE is embedded in structured light methods to address underexposure problems in coded strip-patterns images' underexposure settings. Experiments show that our CSIE outperforms the existing used for natural image-enhanced methods in terms of 2D enhancement, point clouds extraction (at least 17% improvement), and 3D model reconstruction.

  • Semantic-Guided Variational Model for Sealing Nail Defect Extraction within Albedo Domain (from uncalibrated photometric stereo (UPS) technology) - 项目主导人

    • Project Page

    Automatic 2D vision-based defect detection on sealing nail (SealN) surfaces is challenging due to interference of complex backgrounds with non-homogeneous and low contrast between foreground and background. Inspired by an interesting observation that the albedo domain recovered by the uncalibrated photometric stereo (UPS) shows obvious differences and significant abruptness between defects’ and non-defects’ regions, we develop a novel semantic-guided variational model (SGVM) to conditional extract structural defects from albedo map. Specifically, SGVM utilizes one developed global regularized label indicator to semantically guide one local regularized relative Gaussian filter (RGF) for achieving large-scale structures (i.e., defects) preservation and small-scale textures (i.e., background) suppression. Furthermore, defects can be efficiently extracted by thresholding the structure map within the label indicator. Additionally, experimental results on numerous challenging defect images reveal that the proposed SGVM outperforms the existing advanced 2D methods in terms of defect extraction.

  • HDR Video Generation for Traditional Surveillance Video in the Radiance Domain - 主要研究人员

    • A novel method has been developed to generate high dynamic range (HDR) video without using multi-exposures or requiring changes to current video systems. The key to determining the camera response function (CRF) is through 24h varying illuminated images. Additionally, images are processed in the radiance domain, which has achieved better results than conventional intensity-domain image processing. This approach follows the imaging pipeline and avoids saturation that can result from wide dynamic range scenes.
